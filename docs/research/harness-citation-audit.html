The real story (and the fix)

What actually went wrong

Neither the roadmap nor the audit shows any sign of having verified these citations before declaring the goal “unusually well-grounded.” The papers being real is epistemic luck, not diligence — and the audit thereby committed the same sin it accuses the harness of in its own Finding B: confident claims without cited evidence.

The proportionate remedy

Don’t retract the conclusion — it verified true. Instead: fix the HarnessFix title, demote the formula to a restatement, add HTIR + the prior-art lineage, and adopt a one-line citation-provenance rule (“*Verified <date> via <source>*”) — the doc-layer analogue of the evaluator’s evidence-or-FAIL discipline.
